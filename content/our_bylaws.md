## **Bylaws of the Youngstown Action Center**  

### **ARTICLE I: NAME OF ORGANIZATION**

The name of the organization is Youngstown Action Center. Throughout this document it may be referred to as “The Organization”, “The Youngstown Action Center”, or “The YAC”

### **ARTICLE II: CORPORATE PURPOSE**

#### **Section 1: Nonprofit Purpose**

This corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes, including, for such purposes, the making of distributions to organizations that qualify as exempt organizations under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code.  

#### **Section 2: Specific Purpose**  

Youngstown Action Center (YAC) is a fiscally sponsored collective that organizes and co-organizes events and campaigns throughout the Mahoning Valley and maintains a movement space used for meetings, events, office work, co-work, library, storage, and other community based projects in line with our values.

### **ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP**

Membership of Youngstown Action Center is limited to the members of the Board of Directors.

The Board of Directors is made up of representatives of Organizations that make up a coalition of affinity groups and project partners that have taken on stewardship of the space (107 Park Ave) known as the Youngstown Action Center.

The Board of Directors will have a number of Officers and Committees that will be elected from among their ranks.

### **ARTICLE IV: THE YAC COALITION**

#### **Section 1: Overview**

The YAC Coalition or The Coalition will henceforth refer to the collection of Organizations and Project Partners (groups) which have come together and elected representatives from among their ranks to take up stewardship of the space known as The Youngstown Action Center. The Coalition is made by the free association of affinity groups that have worked together for an amount of time that they all feel comfortable with. The Coalition is made up of Organizations that have determined that the overall goals, strategies, and structures are in alignment with each other and who have developed a strong and mutually beneficial working relationship. The Coalition will be a non-hierarchical structure and will not have officers, committees, or other formal structures of its own.

#### **Section 2: Responsibilities**

The Coalition and its member Organizations and Partners will have the general responsibility of electing members from among their ranks to carry on the day to day organizing and management of the Youngstown Action Center. Organizations and Partners will have the autonomy to decide their own methods for electing such representatives, and will be charged with fully informing those representatives of the responsibilities that come with being a Member of the Board (These are described below). Members of The YAC Coalition will maintain channels of communication and inform each other of changes that will affect their responsibilities to The Board of Directors in a timely fashion

#### **Section 3: Coalition Membership**

Membership in The Coalition will be made available to groups based on the following criteria:

- They will have worked with the YAC Coalition and in The YAC space long enough to make themselves and the majority of their members known to the existing groups in The Coalition and the majority of their members

- Their mission and the work they do in the community will have been examined by enough members of the existing groups that make up the YAC Coalition that the existing members are able to make an informed decision that the prospective group’s work and mission is in alignment with the work and missions of the existing YAC Coalition members.

- The prospective group will have determined to have some sort of demonstrable behavior of stake in assisting in helping the Youngstown Action Center grow and function

- The current Coalition Members will give the prospective members a formal application that will be filled out and resubmitted to the Board and its respective groups/members  
    
- The prospective member will review the Points of Unity, the Bylaws, and other relevant documents and agree to be bound by them

Upon meeting the above criteria, The Board of Directors will call a Special Meeting and place a motion to expand the Board to create up to two seats for a potential new Coalition Member at the beginning of the next calendar year. This Special Meeting will be open to the general membership of the Organizations and Groups that make up the Coalition. Members of The Board of Directors will inform their respective Organizations or Project Partners of the change and allow for members of their group that aren’t on the board to voice concerns, support or opposition to the proposal. At the next Regular Meeting of the Board of Directors, the Board will decide via consensus to allow for the addition of the new Coalition Member.

### **ARTICLE V: BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

#### **Section 1: General Powers**

The board is responsible for overall policy and direction of the association, and delegates responsibility of day-to-day operations to committees. The board will have the power to create committees and sanction YAC projects. The board will have the power to maintain the space and a responsibility to do so. The board shall be made up of up to two (2) representatives from each of the groups that make up the YAC Coalition. The board receives no compensation other than reimbursement for reasonable and documented expenses

The Board of Directors will also work to schedule and maintain office hours in an effort to be available for public drop ins.

#### **Section 2: Elections and Terms**

All elections and appointments to the Board shall be for two year terms. Coalition members and project partners will select representatives from their own ranks Coalition members and project partners will select representatives from their own ranks . No person shall serve more than three consecutive terms unless a majority of the Board at a Board meeting at which a quorum is present votes to appoint a Board member to one additional term. No person shall serve more than four consecutive terms , unless decided by total consensus (minus the person in question) by The Board to continue. After serving to their term limit, a person  may be eligible for reconsideration as a Board member after two years have passed since the conclusion of their service.

To qualify to be a Board Member, an individual shall be at least eighteen (18) years of age and an active member of a group or project within the YAC coalition. Prospective Coalition Partners and Project Partners will be approved by the Board via the outlined process in ARTICLE IV before electing their Board Members. The Board Member must also not be under current sanction by any YAC space member organization.

#### **Section 3: Regular Meetings**

The board shall meet at least quarterly, and will make best efforts to meet monthly, at an agreed upon time and place. An official board meeting requires that each board member have written notice at least two weeks in advance.

A written agenda will be made available to all Board Members in advance of all regular meetings. The Secretary will send out the agenda no less than a week before the meeting, but will make efforts to have the agenda available as far in advance of the meeting as possible. Board Members shall have the opportunity to propose agenda items to the Secretary who will add them to the agenda.

A Facilitator shall preside at all meetings of the board. The Facilitator for all board meetings shall be selected by the board at the start of the meeting. The Board will also select someone to take down the meeting minutes.

#### **Section 4: Special and Emergency Meetings**  

Special meetings of the board shall be called upon the request of the chair, or one-third of the board. Notices of special meetings shall be sent out by the secretary to each board member at least two weeks in advance.

An Emergency Meeting would be a subset of “Special Meetings” called in response to an event that requires a quick response from The Board. An emergency meeting may be called by The Executive committee, or one-third of The Board. An agenda will be sent out to The Board that will focus on the event that created the need for the Emergency Meeting. An Emergency Meeting should be scheduled no farther out than 2 weeks from when it was called.  

A written agenda which states all matters that require the attention of an Emergency or Special Meeting of the Board will be given to the board at the earliest possible time. In addition to the written agenda, any supporting documents to be reviewed will also be given to the Board Members at the earliest possible time.

#### **Section 5: Quorum**

At each meeting of the Board or Board Committees, the presence of more than  50% +1 members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Any members not present at the meeting, shall be given the right to vote on business via electronic means or a special meeting to ensure consensus process.

If at any time the Board fails to reach consensus, a vote may be called requiring weak consensus, which will be 60% of the vote. The act of the majority of the Board members serving on the Board or Board Committees and present at a meeting in which there is a quorum shall be the act of the Board or Board Committees, unless otherwise provided by the Articles of Incorporation, these Bylaws, or a law specifically requiring otherwise. If a quorum is not present at a meeting, the Board members present may adjourn the meeting from time to time without further notice until a quorum shall be present.

#### **Section 6: Attendance**

An elected Board member who is absent from three consecutive regular meetings of the Board during a calendar year is encouraged to re-evaluate with the Board Chair their commitment to YAC. The Board may deem a Board member who has missed three consecutive meetings without such a re-evaluation with the Chair to have resigned from the Board.

Any board meeting may be conducted solely by one or more means of remote communication through which all directors may participate in the meeting, if notice of the meeting is given as described in Section 3 and if the number participating is sufficient to constitute a quorum as described in Section 5. Remote communication includes but is not limited to telephone, video, the Internet, or such other means by which persons may communicate with each other on a substantially simultaneous basis. Participation in a meeting by any of the above-mentioned means constitutes attendance at a meeting.

#### **Section 7: Vacancies**

When a vacancy on the board exists mid-term, the secretary must receive notification of new members from the previous Board Member’s respective Organization three weeks in advance of a regular meeting. These vacancies will be filled only to the end of the particular board member’s term.

#### **Section 8: Confidentiality**

YAC is committed to respecting the privacy and confidentiality of its members, especially during virtual meetings online. We ask for explicit consent for sharing of any confidential information. Explicit consent for confidential information shall be sought by request to an executive committee member, who will return a decision within 48 hours during the business week (9am-5pm Monday through Friday).

“Explicit consent” will also be required from any participating member whose words, voice, or other likeness would be released in the act of sharing any information.

This section shall also mandate using, when available and feasible, the most secure forms of communication & physical/data storage.

#### **Section 9: Decision Procedure**

The Executive Committee decides by consensus as a first resort. If consensus cannot be achieved, the board or the Executive Committee may use a weak  consensus(60%) vote. The board decides after quorum has been achieved.

#### **Section 10: Resignation**

Each Board member shall have the right to resign at any time upon written notice thereof to the Board Chair, Secretary of the Board, or the Executive Director. Unless otherwise specified in the notice, the resignation shall take effect upon receipt thereof, and the acceptance of such resignation shall not be necessary to make it effective.

#### **Section 11: Removal**

Resignation from the board must be in writing and received by the secretary. A board member may be terminated from the board due to excess absences, more than three no call/no show absences from board meetings in a year. A board member may be removed for other reasons by the process outlined below.

Any Member of the Board of Directors may be removed at any time with Cause by the remaining members of the Board. To initiate removal proceedings, a proposal shall be submitted to the Secretary to add the removal of a Member of the Board to the Agenda of the next Regular Board Meeting. If the Secretary is the Board Member being removed, the proposal will instead be submitted to the Chairperson. A Special or Emergency Meeting may also be called to initiate these proceedings. The Executive Committee will come to consensus on whether or not to add the proposal to the agenda. If a member of the Executive Committee is having these procedures initiated about them, they will not be included in the discussion or decision making process for the proposal. Once the Proposal is added to the agenda, the Executive Committee will notify the Board Member in question of the agenda item. The Board of Directors will discuss the proposal and will follow the consensus process to remove the Member in question. The Member in question will not be allowed to take part in the consensus process but may be called on for comments if the Board so chooses.

#### **Section 12: General Assembly**

A General Assembly is an open assembly of non-voting members of YAC programs or partners, as well as affiliated groups and organizations that are seeking to align their work and progressive action in the Mahoning Valley. A General Assembly will be called at least once a year, and The Board will make best efforts to call for a General Assembly twice yearly. The Board shall determine the medium the general assembly will use (ie, virtual, hybrid, in person) and will make efforts to make the General Assembly open and accessible to all. Two weeks prior to the meeting a report back of YAC and affiliated groups’ work, current programs, and next six month forecast will be shared, and an agenda opened. After the meeting, notes will be sent out within a week. The General Assembly will allow space for members of affiliate groups and community members to make suggestions to The Board heard and for an open discussion on project trajectories so as to allow affinity groups to attempt to coordinate and collaborate.

### **ARTICLE VI. OFFICERS**

The officers of this Board shall be the Chairperson, Assistant Chairperson, Secretary and Treasurer. All officers must have an active membership in the organization. All board members should also be members of at least one committee.

#### **Section 1: Chairperson**

The Chairperson shall preside at all meetings of the membership. The Chairperson shall have the following duties

- calling meetings

- de facto facilitator if none other available

- spokesperson for group if none other available

- stewardship of mission and recruitment of potential new coalition members

#### **Section 2: Assistant Chairperson**

The Assistant Chairperson shall perform all the duties of the Chairperson during the absence of the latter. The Assistant Chairperson duties are:

- call meetings if chairperson is unavailable

- head facilitator of grievance policy unless actively involved

- responsible for primary stewardship of the YAC space /environs

#### **Section 3. Secretary**

The Secretary shall attend all meetings of the General Assembly and of the Executive Committee, and all meetings of The Board . The Secretary’s duties shall consist of:

- compile notes and archive correctly

- receives proposed agenda items for meetings from board members

- sends out agenda for meetings in required timeframe

- compile committee reports and action reports for board meetings

- maintains YAC email and routes emails to correct person/role

- maintains information about governance procedures

- govern elections

#### **Section 4. Treasurer**

The Treasurer’s duties shall be:

- maintaining relationship with fiscal sponsor

- inputting all expenses

- provide budget reports at each board meeting

- ensuring reimbursements in a timely fashion

- train board members and others on expense procedure

- use fiscal sponsor credit card

- works with fundraising committee for budgets

- ensuring grant reports are submitted on time and in complete

#### **Section 5. Election of Officers**

Elections for officers shall be held at the first Regular Meeting of the calendar year unless otherwise stated in these bylaws. The board shall open nominations no less than thirty (30) days prior to the election and call for nominations to the membership.

The secretary shall present nominations for new and renewing Board members at the Board meeting immediately preceding the beginning of the next fiscal year. Recommendations from the Executive Committee shall be made known to the Board in writing before nominations are made and voted on. New and renewing Board members shall be approved by a majority of those Board members at a Board meeting at which a quorum is present.

All officers shall be members of the Board during their terms of office. Officers shall be elected for a one year term. No officer shall be eligible to serve more than two consecutive terms in the same office. The officers of the Board shall be elected annually by the Board at regular Board meetings as terms expire or vacancies otherwise arise. A vacancy occurring in any office due to death, resignation, removal, disqualification, or any other cause shall be filled by consensus appointment of the board.

### **ARTICLE VII. COMMITTEES**

#### **Section 1: Committee Formation**

The board may allow the creation of standing or ad hoc committees as needed, such as fundraising, finance, etc. Ad hoc committees must be made with a specific stated purpose that includes an end goal or a specified expiration date. Standing committees will require an amendment to the Bylaws to include the new committee, its functions, and its makeup.

#### **Section 2: Standing Committees**

The Board shall maintain the following standing committees: Executive Committee, Finance Committee, Outreach Committee, May Day Committee, Interior Committee. Committees will select a chair person for the purposes of giving status reports to The Board during meetings and for the purposes of giving status reports to the General Assembly.

In the event that a vacancy opens on any standing committee, the Executive Committee will make recommendations to the Board of Directors for a person/persons to fill the vacancy. The Board of Directors will approve the recommendations after review at the next Regular Meeting.

Committees may adopt rules and self govern themselves in a manner that does not conflict with the Bylaws of the YAC or rules adopted by the Board of Directors. Quorum will be considered met by a majority of committee members in attendance at a committee meeting.

#### **Section 3: Executive Committee**

The executive committee shall be made up by the Chairperson, The Assistant Chairperson, The Secretary, and the Treasurer.

The Executive Committee will be charged with daily governance and minutia of the Youngstown Action Center space. They will also be the primary emergency contacts for the space and will be charged with the first line of rapid response for emergent community events that require the Youngstown Action Center. The Executive Committee will be allowed to create ad hoc committees with consensus-based approval from the board.

Once per calendar year, the Executive Committee will conduct a review of the Bylaws to ensure that the Bylaws fit in accordance with the mission and spirit of the Youngstown Action Center and the coalition that makes it.

#### **Section 4: The Finance Committee**

The Finance Committee shall be composed of three or more Board members, and will be chaired by The Treasurer. The Finance Committee shall oversee all financial operations of the organization, develop long-range fiscal plans, procure and review any and all external audits, and prepare and recommend an annual operating budget to the Board. Responsible for developing fundraising strategies in support of the organization’s mission, including by supporting fundraising event targets. This includes grants, direct asks, fundraising events, and fundraising components of other events and actions.

#### **Section 5: The Outreach Committee**

The Outreach Committee shall be composed of three or more members and will be chaired by the Chairperson. This committee shall focus on raising overall awareness about YAC in the Valley community as well as supporting the YAC board and orgs. This committee builds, enacts, and engages with communications and marketing for YAC, its projects and events. This includes internal communications such as newsletters and reminders as well as external communications like the website, social media and promotional graphics.

#### **Section 6: May Day Committee**

The May Day Committee shall bottom line the production of the annual May Day Festival, including holding public meetings, preparing budgets, and working with other committees to ensure a successful event in line with the mission. This Committee will be made up of at least three members, one of whom must be a board member.

#### **Section 7: Interior Committee**

The Interior Committee shall manage and maintain improvements and repairs to the YAC, will be chaired by the Assistant Chairperson, maintain the YAC Calendar, and approve additional/changed use of rooms/environments. This Committee will have a permanent spot reserved for the Library Curator. Maintenance and updating of the Library will fall under the purview of the Interior Committee, but will be specifically managed by the Library Curator. In the event that the Library Curator leaves the YAC, the Interior Committee will nominate a new person to be the Library Curator upon approval from the Board of Directors.

### **Article VIII: Projects and Partners**

#### **Section 1: Partner and Project Responsibilities**

All YAC sponsored projects and YAC building sub-tenants will be familiar with the YAC mission, history, and programs, and adhere to the points of unity, alcohol policy, weapons policy, community agreements, and other internal documents.

Projects and tenants may add more requirements, as long as they don’t contradict existing rules and norms.

#### **Section 2: Partners**

YAC building partners are groups/organizations/individuals who use the YAC space (107 Park Ave) and thus adhere to YAC space policy when on site (alcohol, weapons etc). They maintain autonomous governance structures, finance, and membership. We can freely enter and exit coalitions and co-promotion of events, actions, campaigns, and programs.

#### **Section 3: Projects**

YAC maintains fiscal sponsorship of a number of ongoing group formations. These are “YAC Projects,” which function in equivalent fashion to YAC standing committees. These projects must adhere to YAC bylaws as pertains to points of unity, finance reporting, and participation in committees. They may have their own autonomous decision-making structures as outlined in the “standing committees”

### **Article IX: Finances**

#### **Section 1: Fiscal Year.**

The fiscal year of YAC shall be from January 1st to December 31st.

#### **Section 2: Annual Budget**

The Board shall adopt an annual operating budget, which specifies major expenditures by type and amount.

#### **Section 3: Books and Records**

YAC shall keep correct and complete books and accounting records and shall also keep minutes of the proceedings of its Board. The Finance Committee will be responsible for keeping the books and doing regular audits of the finances. At least once per fiscal year, the Board of Directors will review the Books and Records for the Organization.

Minutes will be kept at all meetings of the Board of Directors and its Standing Committees. Meeting Minutes will be submitted to the Secretary once they are completed. The Secretary will compile and archive meeting minutes in such a way that they can be easily recalled at the request of the Board of Directors or any of its Officers. Additionally, meeting minutes, and other YAC records will be available to the public upon request. The Executive Committee’s meeting minutes will be available to the Board of Directors but not to the general public.

#### **Section 4: Contracts and Grants**

The Board may authorize any officer(s) or agent(s) of YAC to enter into contracts, leases, and agreements with and accept grants and loans from any State or international structure; its departments and agencies; the State of Ohio; its agencies, counties, municipalities, and political subdivisions; and public or private corporations, foundations, and persons; and may generally perform all acts necessary for a full exercise of the powers vested in it. The Chairperson shall, at the direction of the board, enter into such contracts and expend such funds on behalf of the organization

#### **Section 5: Checks, Drafts, or Orders for Payment**

All checks, drafts, or orders for the payment of money, notes, or other evidences of indebtedness issued in the name of YAC shall be signed by such officer(s) or agent(s) of YAC and in such manner as shall from time to time be determined by resolution of the Board. In the absence of such determination by the Board, such instruments shall be signed by the Executive Director and co-signed by the Treasurer.

#### **Section 6: Deposits**

All funds of YAC shall be deposited to our fiscal sponsor.

#### **Section 7: Acceptance of Gifts**

The Board may accept on behalf of YAC any cash contribution, gift, bequest, or devise for the general purposes, or for any special purpose, of YAC. Prior to acceptance of a significant non-cash contribution, gift, bequest, or devise, the Board shall determine, by resolution thereof, that the acceptance of such non-cash contribution, gift, bequest, or devise by YAC would be consistent with and further the purposes of YAC. If/when a Board Member receives a gift on behalf of the YAC they will use appropriate communication channels to inform the rest of the Board of Directors as soon as possible, and the Treasurer and/or the Finance Committee will update their records accordingly.

### **ARTICLE X. AMENDMENTS**

#### **Section 1: Amending the Bylaws**

From time to time, the Bylaws for the Youngstown Action Center may need to be amended to reflect changes in the organization and allow for better functioning of the Organization. In recognition of this, the following process is outlined for amending the Bylaws.

Any member of the Board of Directors may initiate or attempt to initiate a change to the Bylaws. This will be done by the Member of the Board submitting a written proposal to the Secretary to have their proposed change added to the agenda of the next Regular Meeting of the Board of Directors. This written proposal will also include the language to be changed and the updated language to be added. The Secretary will add the proposal to the agenda for the next Regular Meeting of the Board. The proposed changes will then be reviewed and discussed by the Board of Directors. Once fully discussed and reviewed, the Board will follow the consensus process to achieve consensus on the passage or failure of the proposal. Should the proposal pass via consensus, the Executive Committee will update the Bylaws to reflect the updated language. The Board of Directors may choose to table the proposal should they determine they need more time to discuss the changes.

The Executive Committee or One Third of the Board of Directors (via written notice to the rest of the Board of Directors) may call for an Emergency Meeting of the Board of Directors with the agenda of amending the Bylaws via the process above if it is determined that immediate changes to the Bylaws are necessary for the continued day-to-day functioning of the Youngstown Action Center. This meeting will follow the outlined procedures for an Emergency Meeting as well as the procedures outlined in the paragraph above.

During their yearly review of the Bylaws, the Executive Committee may come across old and/or antiquated language in the Bylaws. Should the Executive Committee determine that language changes and updates are needed to reflect the on the ground reality of the Organization, the Executive Committee will write a proposal following the process above and submit it to the agenda for the next Regular Meeting of the Board of Directors.

#### **Section 2: Amending the Articles of Incorporation**

From time to time, it may become necessary to amend the Article of Incorporation. In the case that the Board of Directors find the need to amend the Articles of Incorporation, they should initiate the same proceeding outlined above, but instead of listing changes to the Bylaws, the verbiages in question will belong to the Articles of Incorporation. The same consideration for meeting and initiation procedures should be used to set an agenda for the Articles of Incorporation as would be used for the Bylaws.
